## 📌 Project Overview

This is a **simulated Business Analyst portfolio project** built to demonstrate 
end-to-end BA skills across the full project lifecycle, from requirements 
gathering to data analysis to strategic recommendations.

**The scenario:** Etheria is a fictional phygital fashion-tech brand operating 
across 7 countries with 600+ employees. The brand combines augmented reality, 
gamification, and fashion to create immersive shopping experiences. Following 
rapid post-2020 expansion, the HR Head raises a concern — rising burnout, 
declining mental wellbeing, and inconsistent productivity across remote, hybrid, 
and onsite teams, with no structured mechanism to diagnose why.

**My role:** I took on the role of the Business Analyst engaged by Etheria's 
HR Head to investigate, analyse, and deliver a data-backed People & Wellbeing 
Strategy. Every deliverable in this project — including the Client Brief, BRD, SQL analysis, 
Power BI dashboard, and Gap Analysis — was created to simulate a real-world consulting engagement 
using Agile and Business Analysis practices.

For this project, a representative 100-employee sample dataset was analysed to identify
burnout drivers and workplace wellbeing patterns.

## 🔍 Key Findings

- **Sleep is the #1 burnout driver** — poor sleepers average 53.89 burnout vs 47.33 
  for good sleepers, a gap of 6.56 points
- **Remote work causes structural burnout** — remote employees with Good mental health 
  still report the highest burnout at 62.73, pointing to boundary and routine problems
- **Hybrid is the optimal work model** — lowest burnout (47) and highest productivity (6.27)
- **India vs Australia gap is critical** — 28.18-point burnout difference between 
  same-sized country groups requiring urgent investigation
- **Therapist access reflects reverse causality** — higher burnout employees seek therapy; 
  therapy is not preventing burnout
- **QA Engineers are at highest risk** — 45% report Poor mental health, the highest of 
  any role

## ⚠️ Limitations & Future Scope

- Dataset covers 100 employees — findings should be validated against the full 
  600+ employee dataset before policy implementation
- Working hours vs burnout finding was statistically inconclusive at this sample size 
  and requires re-analysis with the complete dataset
- Gap Analysis was not formally tracked in Jira — identified as a process improvement 
  for future projects
